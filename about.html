Cervical Cancer Screening

NEEDS INNOVATION

Cervical cancer devastates low and middle-income countries (LMICs). 90% of the 528,000 cases and 266,000 deaths

from cervical cancer in 2012 occurred in LMICs. A lack of proper screening techniques will contribute to cervical

cancer causing 447,000 deaths in LMICs in 2030. This global disparity in cervical cancer screening is unacceptable.

Visual inspection with acetic acid (VIA) testing has become the standard for cervical cancer screening in LMICs

because of its accessibility and low price. For the test, clinicians apply 3% vinegar to the ectocervix and inspect it

after 3 minutes for precancerous lesions whitened by the vinegar. VIA shows low diagnostic sensitivity (67-79%)

and specificity (49-86%) due to inconsistent lighting conditions in clinics and inadequate lesion detection training.

The cerVIA System

REVOLUTIONIZES CERVICAL CANCER SCREENING

TECHNOLOGY

The cerVIA system integrates a custom camera system with a unique cervical cancer lesion detection algorithm in a

speculum-fitted device to address problems with VIA in LMICs. The cerVIA system seamlessly integrates into the VIA

workflow and fits within standard speculums. The custom camera system standardizes input images for lesion detection.

These images feed into an algorithm in our Android application that detects precancerous lesions. The algorithm then

outputs heatmaps to highlight problem areas that clinicians can use to make more-informed diagnoses.

PROGRESS

During rigorous preliminary testing, the cerVIA algorithm shows an increased diagnostic sensitivity and specificity to 91.7%

and 88.6%, respectively, marking a ~23% improvement over conventional VIA. Through the Columbia Business School's Lean

LaunchPad and IE@Columbia Programs, our team has constructed a comprehensive business model centered around our

target market of South India. This business model, coupled with the efficacy of our technology has led our team to win

significant pre-seed funding from the 2016 Columbia Venture Competition. Currently, Luso Labs is continuing to optimize our

system's algorithm while planning our upcoming clinical trial in India and pursuing additional funding opportunities.
